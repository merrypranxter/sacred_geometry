## Circle Packing

### Hexagonal Close Packing (Flower of Life arrangement)
- Most efficient 2D packing of equal circles
- Packing density: π/(2√3) ≈ 0.9069 (90.69% of plane covered)
- Each interior circle touches exactly 6 others
- Coordination number: 6

### Why 6?
```
360° / 60° = 6
The equilateral triangle has 60° angles.
The Vesica Piscis creates 60° angles.
6 × 60° = 360° = one full rotation.
Six circles fit perfectly around one central circle of the same radius.
This is why the Flower of Life has 6-fold symmetry.
```

## Platonic Solid Relationships

### Duality
Each Platonic solid has a dual (connecting face centers gives another Platonic solid):

```
Tetrahedron  ↔  Tetrahedron (self-dual!)
Cube         ↔  Octahedron
Dodecahedron ↔  Icosahedron
```

### Euler's Formula
For ALL convex polyhedra: **V - E + F = 2**

| Solid | V | E | F | V-E+F |
|-------|---|---|---|-------|
| Tetrahedron | 4 | 6 | 4 | **2** |
| Cube | 8 | 12 | 6 | **2** |
| Octahedron | 6 | 12 | 8 | **2** |
| Dodecahedron | 20 | 30 | 12 | **2** |
| Icosahedron | 12 | 30 | 20 | **2** |

### Symmetry Groups
```
Tetrahedron:             T_d  (24 symmetries)
Cube + Octahedron:       O_h  (48 symmetries)
Dodecahedron+Icosahedron: I_h  (120 symmetries)
```

The icosahedron/dodecahedron group (120 symmetries) is related to the 
120-cell in 4D space and the E8 lattice. This is where sacred geometry 
meets cutting-edge theoretical physics.

### Platonic Solid ↔ Element (Plato's Assignment)
```
Tetrahedron  → Fire    (fewest faces, "sharpest")

Octahedron   → Air     (held between fingers, spins freely)
Icosahedron  → Water   (most sphere-like, flows)
Dodecahedron → Cosmos  (12 faces = 12 zodiac signs; Plato: "God used for the all")
```

## The Sri Yantra Constraint

The Sri Yantra's 9 triangles must satisfy a system of equations that is 
**overdetermined** — more constraints than unknowns. Only specific discrete 
families of solutions exist.

The mathematical problem: Given 9 triangles (4 upward, 5 downward), find 
angles such that every intersection point involves exactly 3 lines meeting 
at a single point (no "near misses" forming tiny micro-triangles).

This was proven computationally in the 1980s. The solution angles are 
transcendental numbers — they cannot be expressed as simple fractions of π.

## Phyllotaxis and the Golden Angle

Plants space their leaves, seeds, and petals by the **golden angle**:
```
Golden angle = 360° / φ² = 360° × (1 - 1/φ) ≈ 137.508°
```

Why? Because this angle is the most "irrational" — it shares no common 
factor with 360°. This prevents leaves from stacking above each other, 
maximizing light exposure.

Fibonacci numbers appear in spiral counts because when you pack points 
using the golden angle, the nearest-neighbor connections form spirals whose 
counts are consecutive Fibonacci numbers.

```
Sunflower:  34 + 55 spirals  (Fibonacci: F(9) + F(10))
Pinecone:   8 + 13 spirals   (Fibonacci: F(6) + F(7))
Daisy:      21 + 34 spirals  (Fibonacci: F(8) + F(9))
```
